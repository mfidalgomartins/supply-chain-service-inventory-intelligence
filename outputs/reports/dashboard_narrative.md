# Dashboard Narrative (Concise)

The network is operating at a **92.27% fill rate**, which indicates a workable baseline, but risk is concentrated in high-value failure pockets that leadership should not treat as noise. Observed stockout exposure remains material at **7.73%**, with **EUR 34.78M** lost sales and **961,872 units** of service gap versus policy targets.

Service pressure is concentrated in **WH-LYON** and **WH-MAD**, while category loss is led by **Health** and **Pet Care**. Supplier instability is a key driver: **SUP-002** combines weak OTD (**41.17%**) and high delay variability, with **EUR 13.54M** of downstream lost-sales exposure.

Inventory is also inefficient in parallel: DOS tail risk is high (p90 **50.53**, p99 **148.29**), and excess value concentration is strongest in **Household** and **Health**. Observed trapped working-capital proxy is **EUR 321.37M**.

Management priority is to run a dual-track plan: recover service in constrained lanes while releasing capital from structurally overstocked segments. Estimated 12-month opportunity proxy is **EUR 42.79M**; this is directional and assumption-dependent, not an accounting forecast.
